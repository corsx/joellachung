Brand identity design for Upper Crust Bakery

Tools: Adobe Illustrator | Student Project

The branding identity of Upper Crust Bakery captures the bakery's vision of being an inviting and homely place for people of all ages to visit. Through a warm color palette, rough lines and texture, vintage-like font, and imagery of a fresh loaf of bread served on a platter, the designs exhibit a vivid presentation of the bakery's long, established history and amiable presence. The carefully designed logo and cohesive pieces of the logo and business card were crafted in Illustrator, and the latter was made print-ready with bleed, printer's marks, and CMYK color format.

Interactive Magazine for Caliber

Tools: Adobe InDesign | Student Project

Vertical Layout

Horizontal Layout

An interactive magazine designed for Caliber, a ficitional video game magazine company. Designed in Adobe XD, the magazine has both a vertical and horizontal layout to fit two screen orientations, whether on desktop, tablet, or mobile. The magazine can be viewed digitally on the web or with an eBook software.

The magazine offers a high level of interactivity to give readers a highly engaging and fulfilling experience. Readers can interact with buttons to navigate through the magazine, observe animations, open links, watch videos, view images, and toggle hidden content.

As Caliber's target audience consists of gamers ranging from teens to adults, the design of the magazine appeals to this group and supports the content using a strong, passionate color palette, visually pleasing and functional layout, and striking imagery and graphics.

Website UX Redesign for Toronto Cupcake

Tools: Adobe XD | Student Project

Mobile Prototype

Toronto Cupcake redesign prioritizes usability to offer an enhanced and fulfilling user experience (UX). As the original website design lacks proper UX design, the redesign aims to equip the website with effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction. To identify pain points and weak areas of the original website, user personas were used to understand the target audience and their experience. Such pain points included a hidden and unorganized navigation menu, an inaccessible cart, and a lack of filters to narrow content. The personas were followed by wireframes and mockups that addressed the identified pain points by introducing new features and organizing content to strengthen the UX design. The redesign also retains major elements of the original website that defined the brand, such as the color palette and overall tone.

In upholding the elements of design, the redesign utilizes lines to divide content; typography to create a visual mood of friendliness and honesty; colors to further establish an amiable and playful mood and to create contrast; negative and positive space to enhance readability and simplify the design; contrast using size and color to place emphasis on text; repetition, such as in the menu placement to make viewers feel comfortable and familiar; and harmony through color palettes to create a sense of unity between elements in the website.
