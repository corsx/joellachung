Brand identity design for Upper Crust Bakery

Tools: Adobe Illustrator | Student Project

The branding identity of Upper Crust Bakery captures the bakery's vision of being an inviting and homely place for people of all ages to visit. Through a warm color palette, rough lines and texture, vintage-like font, and imagery of a fresh loaf of bread served on a platter, the designs exhibit a vivid presentation of the bakery's long, established history and amiable presence. The carefully designed logo and cohesive pieces of the logo and business card were crafted in Illustrator and made print-ready with bleed, printer's marks, and CMYK color format.

Magazine layout for Japanese wood-block printing article

Tools: Adobe InDesign | Student Project

Created in Adobe InDesign, two distinct designs show a creative and visually pleasing layout of an article on Japanese wood-block printing. Both layouts take advantage of intriguing images of Japanese wood-block prints and carefully placed pull quotes to break up text and further engage readers in the material. Specifically, the layout exhibits the elements and principles of design by implementing lines to divide content and emphasize certain areas, such as pull quotes; contrast or dominance through the enlargement and color of the text heading and pull quotes; typography using a serif font for readability and to also establish a sophisticated mood; alignment to bring visual interest and order by using left justified type for stronger visuals; and white space by breaking up text and limiting the amount of text and graphics on one page.

The magazine layouts are presented in their best resolution and clarity with implementation of grid and format to properly align text and graphics. Additionally, the layouts are print-ready with the application of accurate colors in the CMYK format as well as proper use of printer's marks, bleed, and margin.

Menu and brochure menu design for TOWN

Tools: Adobe InDesign | Student Project

As TOWN, a Chinese restaurant, bears the vision of being an elegant restaurant for older, high-end clientele, the menu and brochure designs exhibit this through serif fonts, a refined color palette, and a unique layout enhanced by high-quality graphics, geometric shapes, and low opacity values. Specifically, the designs uphold TOWN's sophistication by producing a distinguished mood through serif fonts that suggest class; a black, red and white color palette which taps into the Chinese culture of TOWN and evokes feelings of finesse and grace; balance through lines and shapes and layout of text; white space to ensure that elements have enough room to breathe and to further emphasize elegance; and hierarchy through size and color. The final products are print-ready with attention to bleed, the CMYK color format, and printer's marks.

Responsive Website for Java Been

Mobile Prototype

Website Design and Development for TOWN

Tools: HTML, CSS, JavaScript | Student Project

Live Website

Created using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, the website offers a functional and engaging user experience for visitors of TOWN, a hind-end Chinese restaurant. Website UX Design for Toronto Cupcake

Tools: Adobe XD | Student Project

Mobile Prototype

Toronto Cupcake redesign prioritizes usability to offer an enhanced and fulfilling user experience (UX). As the original website design lacks proper UX design, the redesign aims to equip the website with effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction. To identify pain points and weak areas of the original website, user personas were used to understand the target audience and their experience. Such pain points included a hidden and unorganized navigation menu, an inaccessible cart, and a lack of filters to narrow content. The personas were followed by wireframes and mockups that addressed the identified pain points by introducing new features and organizing content to strengthen the UX design. The redesign also retains major elements of the original website that defined the brand, such as the color palette and overall tone.

In upholding the elements of design, the redesign utilizes lines to divide content; typography to create a visual mood of friendliness and honesty; colors to further establish an amiable and playful mood and to create contrast; negative and positive space to enhance readability and simplify the design; contrast using size and color to place emphasis on text; repetition, such as in the menu placement to make viewers feel comfortable and familiar; and harmony through color palettes to create a sense of unity between elements in the website.

Bird Photography Series

Tools: DSLR Camera, Adobe Lightroom | Student Project

Produced using a DSLR camera and edited in Adobe Lightroom, the Bird Series demonstrates the use of a high shutter speed to capture birds in motion. To compensate for the high shutter speed and maintain proper exposure, a low f/stop and high ISO value were utilized. Additionally, the tools in Adobe Lightroom were used to fine-tune the exposure and adjust the colors to create a stunning capture that accurately reflected the actual scene.

The Bird Series also demonstrates the elements and principles of design through the cropping and alignment of the subject in each shot. For example, the first photo shows emphasis through the placement of the subject and the use of color to make the subject stand out; texture through water in two photographs are highlighted due to the fast shutter speed; color in all photographs to establish a natural and fresh mood; and a mixture of negative and positive in all photographs to create balance and emphasis.

Lakeside Photography Series

Tools: DSLR Camera, Adobe Lightroom | Student Project

Taken using a DSLR camera and edited in Adobe Lightroom, Lakeside Series shows candid moments captured in motion near the lake. To uphold the elements and principles of design, the photograph of a boat resting on the surface of the lake utilizes lines as seen in the divide between the trees and the water; texture in the calm water due to the fast shutter speed; emphasis on the boat through placement and color; and asymmetrical balance by having contrasting elements of the sky and water on each side. The photograph of the woman walking her dogs also shows similar elements and principles, such as leading lines, balance using the trees, and a bright color palette to establish a bright mood.

Still Life Photography Series

Tools: DSLR Camera, Adobe Lightroom | Student Project

Captured with a DSLR camera and edited in Adobe Lightroom, the Still Life Series offers a dramatic close-up of unconventional still life objects with contrasting moods. The large aperture which creates a shallow focus effect emphasizes the objects' excellent textures, and the amount and angle of lighting creates strong values within the photographs to bring out the depth of the objects.

The Still Life Series utilizes the elements and principles of design to create a visually pleasing composition. This includes emphasis using color, value, and aperture; positive and negative space, such as the photograph of the guava and lemon slice which emphasizes minimalism through the strong use of negative space; texture as seen in the subjects skin in both photos and the wood of the chopping board in the photograph of the spider strainer and blueberries; form using lighting to shroud some areas in shadow and others with light; and harmony by combining similar colors and texture for color and texture harmony respectively.
